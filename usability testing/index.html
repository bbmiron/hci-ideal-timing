Overall Objectives

In this usability testing we would like to evaluate how many times spent people on social media and how many content they post. Also, we want to see if our application is easiest to use and helpful for the taget users.

  • Assess the overall effectiveness of the product/application for different types of users performing basic, common tasks;
  • Identify obstacles to completing a certain task or using a certain component;
  • Evaluate consistency and standards;
  • Use the References to guide you in writing your objectives.
Scenario

Scenario for primary Maria

Given that Maria is an internet lover and a very social person and she has expecience with social networks, the IdeaT app will help her to post job description on social networks in an easier way and at a favorable time such that as many people as possible read the ad.

Scenario for secondary Francesca

Francesca will use IdeaT app to promote her content regarding vegan food and campaigns to protect the animals in an easier way. She also post pictures with her original outfits in which she only uses artificial fur. This helps her to have many viewers and make companies reach her for promotion.

Scenario for negative George

George does not have social media accounts, but he could use the mailing features of the application. Some of his enthusiastic colleagues tried to convince him to try to use it, but he thinks that his life experience and what he learned throughout the years about people and his job are more reliable. He often takes his colleagues out for a break from technology to enjoy the life outside of their phones.

Post-test Questions
  • Q1: How easy was it to find the option you needed?
  • Q2: How much do you trust in the recommended date and time?
  • Q3: Do you think is faster than performing the action in the native app?
  • Q4: Do you plan to use IdeaT in the future?
Notes for Test Coordinator
  • Remind the user that you would like them to 'think aloud' so that you can understand what they are thinking, about the task or the product/application;
  • Read the user the scenario for the test;
  • Read the user the tasks he has to complete. If the tasks are complex, you may want to put a sheet of paper in front of the user with each task written out;
  • Record the user's actions, comments, questions, and body language (write them in the Test Observations section accompanied by video/audio).
  • Don't offer help; let the user attempt to perform the task. If they ask for help reply with: "What do you think you/that would do?" "What do you think that means?".

Test Results for: IdeaT Application Component

Success Criteria

A successful design has been achieved when:

  • 100% of users received the confirmation of a successful action
  • 66% of users managed to finish all tasks in the estimated time
  • 66% of users will continue to use the IdeaT app

Summary

  • 3 users observed their action results in the native apps;
  • 1 user perform the action faster than estimated;
  • 1 user finish the action slower than estimated;
  • 2 users found the app easy to use and helpful in their daily life;

Test Observations

User 1: The user was positively surprised by the recommended date and time and how it takes the calendar's events into account. Would have liked to have the option to Login with Linkedin because it is more specific to finding candidates for the job.

User 2: The user thought that the logo was nicely designed. Loved the fact that the number of likes increased because of how the post was scheduled. Would be interested in an Instagram version.

User 3: The user prefered to choose his own date and time because of his work experience. He is slower with typing on phones so would use the app if the email could be inserted by voice.

Potential Design Improvements
  • Integration with Linkedin and Instagram
  • Inserting the posts/emails by voice
